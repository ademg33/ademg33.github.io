PERSPECTIVE WARP. In photography, perspective refers to the relationship between a camera's location in space and the scene being photographed. Depending on the vantage point (the camera angle and camera distance from those elements), objects may appear smaller or larger in a photo than they do to the naked eye. Lines that should be straight may bend a little near the edges of the photo. This is called perspective distortion, and you can fix it with a little photo manipulation using Perspective Warp.
